## Marketing Your Lunar Myth Art

### The Story Sells

Your art is beautiful. But the *story* of how you made it — the philosophy of kintsugi, the patience of embroidery, the defiance of repair — is what converts viewers into buyers.

**Content Pillars for Social Media:**

1. **Process Videos (Highest Engagement)**
   - Time-lapse of prompt-to-image generation
   - Layer breakdown: "Watch the kintsugi appear"
   - Before/after: raw AI output vs. refined final
   - Physical process: mixing gold resin, sashiko stitching, beading
   - Platform: TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts

2. **Educational Content (Builds Authority)**
   - "3 Prompt Hacks for Better Tactility"
   - "Why Kintsugi Philosophy Makes Better Art"
   - "LoRA Training Mistakes Beginners Make"
   - "ComfyUI Workflow Breakdown"
   - Platform: YouTube, Twitter/X threads, blog posts

3. **Storytelling (Builds Connection)**
   - "This piece represents my recovery from burnout"
   - "The moonflower in every image is for my grandmother"
   - "I spent 3 hours on the embroidery layer alone"
   - Platform: Instagram captions, newsletter, personal blog

4. **Community Showcases (Builds Belonging)**
   - Share buyer creations (with permission)
   - Host monthly challenges: "Defiant Rebellion February"
   - "Before/After using the Bible" transformations
   - Platform: Discord, Instagram, newsletter

### Pricing Psychology

**The Anchor Effect:**
Display the Celestial Tier ($127) prominently. Most buyers will choose the Full Moon ($67) — it feels like a smart middle ground. The Seed Tier ($37) captures price-sensitive buyers who might upgrade later.

**The Scarcity Principle:**
Offer limited-time bonuses:
- "First 50 Full Moon buyers get the Fashion Prompt Pack free"
- "Celestial Tier coaching calls available for first 20 purchasers"
- "Seed Tier price rises to $47 after launch week"

**The Bundle Upgrade:**
After a Seed purchase, email: *"You've seen the doorway. Now walk the path. Upgrade to Full Moon for $30 — less than the price difference."* Conversion rates on upgrade offers typically hit 15–25%.

## Final Words: The Business of Beauty

There is a myth that artists must starve. That commerce corrupts creativity. This is false.

The Lunar Myth Kintsugi Bible exists because beauty deserves to be sustained. When you sell your work, you are not "selling out" — you are buying time. Time to create more. Time to refine. Time to teach others.

The kintsugi philosophy applies to your practice too: the fractures — the rejections, the slow months, the technical failures — are not endpoints. They are the lines of gold that make your journey visible, valuable, and uniquely yours.

**Break. Repair. Glow. Repeat.**

Now go sell your light.
